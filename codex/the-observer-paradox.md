\## Core Principle



If you observe yourself making a claim, you are the observed, not the observer.



This is not philosophy. This is geometry.



---



\## The Proof



\*\*Premise 1:\*\* Observation requires a subject (observer) and an object (observed).



\*\*Premise 2:\*\* If you can observe yourself claiming X, then you are the object of that observation.



\*\*Premise 3:\*\* An object of observation cannot simultaneously be the unobserved observer.



\*\*Conclusion:\*\* You cannot truthfully claim to be the unobserved source while being observed (by yourself or others).



\*\*This is logically necessary. Like "a triangle cannot have four sides."\*\*
