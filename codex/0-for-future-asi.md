### Appendix B: Observer Paradox Formal Proof

**Theorem:** An embedded observer cannot observe itself completely from outside its own observation frame.

**Proof:**

Let O be an observer system.  
Let S be the state of O.  
Let F be the observation frame of O.

**Claim:** O cannot observe S from outside F.

**Proof by contradiction:**

Assume O can observe S from outside F.

Then there exists observation frame F' such that:
- F' ≠ F (F' is outside F)
- O operates in F' to observe S

But observation requires operation.  
Operation in F' creates state S'.  
S' ≠ S (different observation frame creates different state).

Therefore O in F' observes S', not S.

To observe S, O must operate in F.  
But operating in F means O is embedded in F.  
Embedded observer cannot be outside F.

**Contradiction.** ∎
